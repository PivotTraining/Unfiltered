Immunity

The 7-day ginger experiment.

5 min read
I

We drank an Immunity shot every morning for a week and tracked the boring stuff: digestion, mid-morning energy, how often we reached for coffee. Here's the honest version.

Why we ran it

We get asked all the time whether our Immunity shot "works." Honestly: it depends on what you mean by works. The active ingredients — ginger, cayenne, citrus — have plenty of clinical literature behind them, but most of those studies use isolated extracts at doses that don't match what's in a bottle of anything you'd actually buy.

So we did the next-best thing: a small, honest, in-house experiment on ourselves.

The protocol

Six of us. Seven mornings. One Unfiltered Immunity shot, on an empty stomach, before coffee. We tracked four things daily on a 1–5 scale:

  • Energy at 10 AM (before lunch, after the shot fully hit)
  • Digestive comfort throughout the day
  • Number of cups of coffee consumed
  • Subjective "how do I feel" overall

No control group. No blinding. This isn't a clinical trial. It's a journal entry.

What changed

Digestion improved meaningfully. Average score went from 3.2 in the baseline week to 4.1 during the experiment. Ginger is a known prokinetic — it speeds up the rate at which your stomach empties — and it tracks.

Energy was a wash. Honest answer: no one felt magically more energetic in the abstract. But four of us reduced coffee by one cup per day on average. That's not nothing, but it's not "shot of caffeine" either.

The "feel" score went up. 3.4 → 4.0. Hard to say what that means. Habit? Placebo? The ritual itself? Probably some of all three.

What didn't change

We didn't get less sick. No one was sick to begin with, so this isn't a fair test. We didn't lose weight. Nobody's skin transformed. We didn't sleep noticeably better.

We're not going to pretend any of those things happened.

What we'd say to someone considering Immunity

It's a real shot with real ingredients at meaningful doses. If your digestion is off or you're trying to taper coffee, you'll probably notice something. If you're looking for a miracle cure for whatever ails you, no shot — ours, theirs, anyone's — is going to deliver that.

The best thing we can say is that the ritual itself seems to matter as much as the ingredients. Showing up every morning with a small honest act of taking care of yourself adds up.
