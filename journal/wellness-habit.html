Ritual

How to build a wellness habit that actually sticks.

3 min read
R

Most morning routines collapse by week two. There's one rule that fixes this — and it's not "drink more water" or "wake up at 5 AM."

Why routines die

Most wellness routines fail for the same reason most diets fail: the bar is set above where you actually live. You design a 90-minute morning for the person you wish you were, not the person you are when the alarm goes off at 6:42 and you forgot to wash your shaker bottle.

The fix is unsexy. It's not motivation. It's not discipline. It's making the friction smaller than the friction of not doing it.

The rule: anchor the small thing to a thing you already do.

This is the one principle that works for us, across every habit we've actually stuck with. You don't need willpower if the thing you're trying to do is bolted to something else you'd do anyway.

Example: "I'll drink a wellness shot every morning" doesn't stick. But "I'll grab a shot when I open the fridge to make coffee" sticks because you were going to open the fridge anyway.

It works for fitness ("when the kettle goes on, I do 20 push-ups"), reading ("when I sit down on the couch, I read 5 pages before unlocking my phone"), even therapy follow-up ("when I get into bed, I write one sentence about today").

Three practical anchors

  • The coffee anchor. Shot first, coffee second. The five seconds it takes to grab a bottle from the fridge are easier than the cognitive cost of remembering later.
  • The brushing anchor. Pair the habit with brushing teeth. Front of the day or back of the day, you're already in the bathroom.
  • The screen anchor. The first time you check your phone, do the habit before you unlock it. Phones are the universal anchor — everyone has one within 90 seconds of waking.

One more trick

Don't aim for "every day." Aim for "I won't miss two in a row." This is the rule that James Clear popularized and we keep coming back to it. Missing once is human. Missing twice is the beginning of stopping. Catching yourself after one miss and getting back on track is the actual habit you're building.

And finally

If you're trying to make our shots part of your morning, here's what works for our subscribers: put them on the front-middle shelf of the fridge, eye level, so you reach past them every time you open the door. Out of sight is out of habit.
